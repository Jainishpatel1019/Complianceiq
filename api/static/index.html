How it works

Four steps from a published regulation to a dollar figure on your desk.

📡

1. Fetch

Every day, a pipeline pulls the latest rules from the Federal Register, FDIC, and Federal Reserve APIs — automatically, no manual reading required.

🔬

2. Detect change

Three mathematical methods measure how much the language changed from the previous version. Small edits score low. New capital requirements score high.

⚖️

3. Measure impact

Causal inference (the same technique used in economics research) estimates how much the regulation will change your bank's risk-weighted assets in dollars.

🤖

4. Explain

An AI agent reads the full regulation and writes a plain-English summary: which business lines are affected, which CFR sections changed, and what to do next.

What do these numbers mean?

Plain-English definitions for every metric on this page.

Risk & Change Detection

Drift Score (0–1)
How different the new regulation text is from the old one. 0 = identical. 1 = completely rewritten. Above 0.15 triggers a review flag.
JSD Score
Jensen-Shannon Divergence — measures how the statistical distribution of words changed. Catches subtle rewording that drift score might miss.
Wasserstein Score
Earth Mover's Distance — measures how far the meaning "moved". High scores mean the topic shifted significantly, not just the wording.
Flagged for Review
Any regulation where the drift score crosses the 0.15 threshold. These are sent to compliance officers for manual review first.
Confidence Interval
The range we're 95% sure the true drift score falls within. A narrow band means the measurement is reliable. A wide band means more data is needed.
ATT Estimate ($M)
Average Treatment Effect — the causal estimate of how much this regulation will change a bank's Risk-Weighted Assets in millions of dollars.
